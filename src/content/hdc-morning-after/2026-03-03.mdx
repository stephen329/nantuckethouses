## Fee Schedule Overhaul

The meeting opened with a public hearing on the updated HDC fee schedule, effective June 1, 2026. The headline change: the 10x multiplier for work done without a permit is now officially listed on the public fee schedule. Previously it was applied internally but never published.

Key details:
- **Demolition fee** increases from $100 to $500 (penalty: $5,000 for unpermitted demolition)
- **Fence without permit**: a $50 application becomes a $500 penalty
- Board directed staff to explore separating enforcement penalties from the fee structure
- As-built applications may get their own fee/enforcement category

The board acknowledged the 10x penalty may still be too low to deter violations on high-value projects. Expect further enforcement discussions in coming months.

## The Cherry Street Decision

The Flagship Club proposal at 1 Cherry St generated the most heated discussion. The new commercial building would replace a 400 sq ft structure with something roughly 7.5 times larger. Two abutters spoke in opposition, citing traffic safety at the Cherry/William intersection, building scale, and sight-line concerns.

The Preservation Planner flagged inconsistencies between commercial and residential plans, recommended 6-over-6 Nantucket-profile windows instead of the proposed 6-over-1 with black trim, and questioned the second-story balcony. Every board member expressed concerns about scale, massing, and OHD compatibility. The unanimous hold requested revised massing, streetscape drawings from three streets, and OHD-appropriate design vocabulary.

## N. Mill Street As-Built Saga Continues

The 8 N. Mill St house move — which came to the board after the fact — drew sustained frustration. The house was moved 5 feet closer to the street, and abutters argued the renderings don't accurately show current conditions. The board requested an on-site mock-up showing plantings, foundation screening, and grading mitigation. Multiple commissioners expressed concern about the precedent of as-built applications, with one calling it an "unfortunate incident."

## Other Notable Decisions

- **17 N. Water St addition**: Approved with praise for the rear massing approach and historic porch bracket details. Board requested the ADA ramp be reconsidered separately with less visible alternatives.
- **6 Coffin St new dwelling**: Held — the hip roof form was deemed out of step with surrounding one-story cottages. Board recommended a gable main block with shorter overall height and actual-grade streetscape drawings.
- **18 Fair St fence and 19 N. Water St chimney**: Both approved with minor conditions.
