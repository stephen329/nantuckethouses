## Meeting Highlights

Spring is officially here, and so is renovation season. This week's HDC meeting featured two significant approvals and one deferral that revealed some evolving commission thinking on ground cover calculations.

### Sconset Compound Renovation

The Shell Street compound project is one of the larger undertakings approved this season. The scope includes exterior restoration of the 1890s main house, structural reinforcement of the guest cottage (which had notable foundation settling), and a comprehensive landscape plan that actually *reduces* impervious surface coverage from the existing condition.

The commission specifically praised the approach of preserving the original cottage proportions rather than expanding the footprint. This is a good template for anyone considering compound work in Sconset.

### Monomoy Pool House

The pool house addition at 9 Monomoy generated thoughtful discussion about accessory structure scale. The approved design keeps the pool house under 400 square feet with a standing-seam roof that matches the main house. The outdoor kitchen component was initially questioned but ultimately approved given the quality of the screening landscape plan.

### Dionis Deferral

A proposed new build on Eel Point Road was deferred pending revisions to the south elevation. The commission asked the architect to reduce the window-to-wall ratio and reconsider the proposed roof pitch, which at 6:12 was considered too low for the surrounding context.
