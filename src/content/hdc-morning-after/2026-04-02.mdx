## Meeting Highlights

A mercifully brief HDC session this week. All three applications were routine renovations with strong documentation and no neighborhood objections.

### Cedar Shingle Update Preview

The most consequential moment was Commissioner Walsh's brief preview of upcoming updates to the cedar shingle installation guidelines. The commission has been working with local contractors to tighten specifications around:

- **Weather exposure**: Moving toward a 5-inch maximum exposure requirement (currently 5.5 inches)
- **Stain colors**: Narrowing the approved palette to better maintain neighborhood consistency
- **Grade requirements**: Potentially requiring #1 Blue Label cedar for all historic district applications

These changes are expected to be formally proposed at the May meeting cycle. Contractors and architects should plan accordingly.

### Window Standards Reminder

The Hussey Street approval included a useful reminder from staff: all replacement windows in the historic district must be true divided light, wood construction. Simulated divided light (SDL) continues to be rejected regardless of quality.
