## Meeting Highlights

Wednesday's HDC session ran long - nearly three hours - as the commission worked through a packed agenda of spring renovation applications. The tone was collegial on the renovation approvals but sharp on the Cliff Road denial.

### The Cliff Road Decision

The proposed new build at 78 Cliff Road generated the most debate. The applicant's architect presented a design that incorporated flat roof sections and large expanses of glass on the south elevation. While the commission acknowledged the quality of materials specified (copper standing seam, mahogany windows), three commissioners expressed concern that the overall massing and fenestration pattern departed too far from the neighborhood's historic character.

The denial was 4-1, with Commissioner Bartlett dissenting, arguing that the design represented a "thoughtful evolution" of island architecture. Expect the applicant to return with revisions in the coming months.

### Renovation Approvals

The Main Street and Orange Street projects sailed through with minimal discussion. Both applications demonstrated careful attention to historic proportions and materials. The Main Street project specifically received praise for its commitment to full cedar shingle replacement rather than partial repairs.

### Looking Ahead

Next week's agenda includes three new construction applications in Mid-Island and a contentious garage conversion in Sconset. The Mid-Island applications should move smoothly given the less restrictive historic overlay in that area.
