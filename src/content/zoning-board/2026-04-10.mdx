## Meeting Highlights

Thursday's ZBA session had a full docket with four items. The height variance denial in Surfside generated the most discussion and reinforces the board's conservative approach to height exceptions.

### Surfside Height Variance Denial

The applicant at 55 Surfside Road sought a variance to build to 38 feet (8 feet above the 30-foot maximum) to accommodate a third-floor observatory room. The board found that the applicant failed to demonstrate the required hardship - the lot is a standard Surfside lot without unusual topography or constraints that would prevent conforming construction.

The vote was unanimous (5-0) to deny. Board chair Morrison noted that height variances require "conditions especially affecting the parcel" and that aesthetic preferences do not constitute hardship under the zoning bylaw.

### Non-Conforming Lot Variances

Both Town variances were granted based on the pre-existing non-conforming status of the lots. The Pine Street addition carefully minimizes the setback encroachment while providing necessary living space. The Step Lane garage was approved with the condition that it include a green roof to partially offset the additional lot coverage.

### Home Office Trend

The Fairgrounds Road home office permit continues the trend of easy approvals for professional office use in residential zones. The board confirmed that the use generates minimal traffic and is consistent with the residential character of the neighborhood.
